IR999 Assigned Handicap System

⚖️ Fair Competition • Expert Evaluation • Balanced Teams

🎯 What is the IR999 Handicap System?

To ensure fair competition, the IR999 Tournament uses an Assigned Handicap model.

🧠 What Does That Mean?

🚫

NOT Based On

  • ❌ GHIN handicaps
  • ❌ USGA ratings
  • ❌ Self-reporting
⚖️

Determined By

  • ✅ Tournament Board
  • ✅ Past performance
  • ✅ Known skill level
  • ✅ Pairing balance

🎯 The Goal:

Create the most competitive and balanced tournament possible by evaluating the strength of each 2-man team and assigning appropriate stroke adjustments.

⚙️ How the System Works

Board Evaluation Process:

  • 📊 Team Assessment: Board evaluates the combined strength of each 2-man team
  • 📈 Historical Data: Past tournament performance and known skill levels
  • ⚖️ Balance Creation: Strokes assigned to level the playing field
  • 🏆 Fair Competition: Every team has a realistic chance to win

📋 Stroke Application:

Full Round Strokes
Applied to total score
NOT Hole-by-Hole
No hole index distribution
Simple Calculation
Final score minus strokes

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I request a specific handicap?

A: Handicaps are determined solely by the Board based on objective evaluation. However, you're welcome to discuss your skill level during registration.

Q: What if I disagree with my team's handicap?

A: You can respectfully discuss concerns with the Board before the tournament. All decisions are made with fairness in mind and are final.

Q: How are strokes applied during different formats?

A: Your team's handicap strokes apply to your final score for each 9-hole segment. The strokes are the same regardless of format (Best Ball, Alternate Shot, or Scramble).

Q: Will handicaps change between tournaments?

A: Yes! The Board continuously evaluates performance and may adjust handicaps for future tournaments to maintain competitive balance.
